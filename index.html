The Doctrine of the Ledger
Lynch · Bogle · Buffett · Pavel Applied to Capital
Capital builds the same way strength does — daily submaximal deposits, never maximal efforts. Pavel's "easy strength" principle applied to money: save as often as possible, as fresh as possible, never to failure. The Bulgarian daily max of the financial life is the automated transfer to the Roth on the 1st of every month. Missed months are like missed training days — each one is compound interest lost forever.
Peter Lynch: "The person who turns over the most rocks wins the game." Apply to both shadowboxing film study and 10-K reading. Saturday's 90-minute research ritual is the financial equivalent of Whitaker tape study.

The Unified Discipline
One Operator · Two Domains · Same Rules
The rules that govern combat training govern capital:
(1) Daily max, not maximal — consistency beats intensity.
(2) Autoregulate — HRV red = reduced training, volatile markets = reduced position size.
(3) Journal every engagement — training log AND trade log, no exceptions.
(4) Three-strike rule — 3 losing trades in a row = session over, same as 3 losses in sparring.
(5) Khalid's reserve — never empty the tank. Keep emergency fund (capital) AND aerobic reserve (body).
The D'Amato mental framework applies directly: "The hero and the coward feel the same thing. The hero uses fear." Fear of loss is the same neurochemistry whether in a trade drawdown or a fifth round. Train the response once; deploy it everywhere.

§ III · The Six Eternal Rules

THE CAPITAL RULES

I
Max Risk Per Trade
Never exceed 1% of account equity. Ever. If sizing feels off — skip. The market prints again tomorrow. Broken accounts do not.
II
Daily Loss Limit
Down 2%? Close platform. Walk away. The rule that keeps the account alive. Khalid's mobile reserve — never empty the tank.
III
A+ Setups Only
Structure + volume + confirmation. If you're explaining it to yourself, it's not a trade — it's a bet. Musashi: "Do nothing that is of no use."
IV
No News Trading
15 minutes before and after red folder events — flat. Spreads widen, stops slip, prop firms hunt kills here.
V
Journal Every Trade
Screenshot, thesis, emotion. Before entry, after exit. No trade exists unless it's inscribed. Ignatian Examen applied to markets.
VI
Three Strike Rule
Three losses in a row — session dead. Stand up. Breathe. Same rule as the 3-strike sparring protocol. Market's not going anywhere.
